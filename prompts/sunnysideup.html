Method



  1. Keep your pan on a medium to low heat and add enough olive or vegetable oil to lightly coat the bottom of a large non-stick pan (about 1 tablespoon).

  2. Crack the eggs into the pan. As the oil gets hotter, you’ll see it start to change the color of the eggs. If the oil starts to spit it’s because it’s too hot, so turn the heat right down. Cook until the tops of the whites are set but the yolk is still runny.

  3. When they’re ready, remove the pan from the heat and take the eggs out using a spatula. Place on a plate and dab them with some paper towels to soak up any excess oil. Serve on toast – no need to butter it – with a sprinkling of freshly ground black pepper.

  4. Delicious served on some 7-grain bread.

Sunny-Side-Up Eggs



Serves: 4
Time: Cooks in 5 minutes



“Perfectly soft and silky fried eggs, ready to eat in just 5 minutes.”



Ingredients


  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/16 teaspoon sea salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste.
